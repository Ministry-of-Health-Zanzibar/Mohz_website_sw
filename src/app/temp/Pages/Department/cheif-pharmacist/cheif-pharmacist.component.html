Chief Pharmacist Office (CPO) and Logistics Management Unit (LMU)

The Chief Pharmacist Office (CPO) oversees the Logistics Management Unit (LMU) responsible for organizing, monitoring, and supporting supply chain activities for all health commodities in Zanzibar.

Vision

To be a center of excellence for supply chain management of health commodities in sub-Saharan Africa by 2020.

Mission

To make available at all times, medicines and medical supplies of acceptable quality and make logistics information accessible at anytime, anywhere through the use of technology.

  • Operational Functions: Managing routine and special activities to run a supply chain, focusing on increasing visibility of logistics data, and coordinating resources to strengthen supply chains for health commodities.

Importance of LMU

The LMU contributes to ensuring that health commodities are available to clients and patients when and where they are needed. Its operational role focuses on ensuring the proper management and availability of supplies for healthcare services.

History and Collaboration

The LMU became fully operational in January 2016. However, due to additional functions in the Chief Pharmacist Office (CPO) related to Policy and Practice, Quality Management, Resource Planning, and Finance, a suggestion was made to rename the LMU to the Supply Chain Management Unit (SCMU). This name change was approved at the 20th ZLTWG meeting.

The Logistics Management Unit plays a vital role in the health sector by ensuring the timely availability of quality health commodities, and its operations are crucial for maintaining an effective healthcare system.
