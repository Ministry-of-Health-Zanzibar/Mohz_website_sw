Directorate of Nursing and Midwifery

Established in 2023 under the leadership of Zanzibar President Dr. Hussein Ali Hassan Mwinyi, the Directorate aims to enhance the quality of nursing and midwifery services and improve the health of the population.

Introduction

The Directorate was officially announced on January 30, 2023, at the Zanzibar Ministry of Health. It addresses the significant role of nursing and midwifery in the healthcare system, including improving care for the elderly, pregnant women, children, and the broader community.

The Directorate comprises three main divisions:

  • Division of Nursing and Midwifery: Oversees nursing services, midwifery services, work ethics, and customer service.
  • Nursing and Midwifery Division: Manages all nursing and midwifery services for the island of Pemba.
  • Division of Standards, Quality and Research: Oversees nursing and midwifery training (self-development and on-job training), clinical research, and publications.

Main Functions

The Department of Nursing and Midwifery is dedicated to:

  • Enhancing the quality of nursing and midwifery services at all levels (public and private).
  • Providing strategic technical advice to the government on nursing and midwifery services within the healthcare system.
  • Promoting evidence-based and standardized nursing and midwifery practices.
  • Developing collaborative partnerships with key stakeholders at national, regional, and international levels.
  • Coordinating nursing and midwifery training (long and short courses) to improve professional skills and deliver quality services to the community.

The department operates under government policies, including the Chama Cha Mapinduzi 2020-2025 manifesto, and aligns with guidelines from the World Health Organization (WHO), the International Council of Nurses (ICN), and the International Confederation of Midwives (ICM).

Mission and Vision

Mission

To provide high-quality nursing and midwifery care with compassion, respect for human dignity, and cultural diversity, meeting local, regional, and international health standards.

Vision

To guide and support nurses and midwives in upholding professional competence, empathy, and commitment to delivering quality services that meet public expectations.
